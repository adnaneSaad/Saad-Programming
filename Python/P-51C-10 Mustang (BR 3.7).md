## 🚀 The 4 Golden Rules of the Mustang Pilot

### 1. Speed is Life, Altitude is Insurance
Never enter a hot engagement zone low and slow. The Mustang is a heavy airframe that requires time to build momentum. If you find yourself below the enemy team's altitude profile, turn 90 degrees away, execute a disciplined side-climb, and engage only when you hold the energy state advantage.

### 2. The 15-Degree Zoom Rule
After diving on an isolated target, **do not turn back horizontally to chase them** if you miss the initial pass. Pitch your nose up into a shallow, sustained 15-degree climb and extend straight out. Lightweight turn-fighters will bleed all their speed attempting to loop up after you, leaving them completely stalled out and vulnerable.

### 3. The Throttle-Cut Overshoot
When a high-speed bandit (such as an early Me 262 jet or a diving super-prop) gets onto your tail at close range, do not panic. Wait until they close to **1.2 km**, drop your throttle to **0%**, and pull a sharp, downward break-turn. The massive unfeathered propeller disc acts as an immediate aerodynamic brake, forcing the faster enemy to overshoot directly into your gunsight.

### 4. Kinetic Laser Discipline
Your six .50 caliber M2 Browning machine guns rely on high muzzle velocity, intense fire rate, and dense stream concentration rather than explosive filler. Set your weapon convergence to **400m or 500m**. Aim precisely for enemy wing roots, fuel tanks, and front engine blocks to tear control cables and initiate catastrophic fires instantly.
